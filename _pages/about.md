I am an NLP researcher at the University of Texas at San Antonio, advised by **Dr. Anthony Rios**. My work explores the boundaries of LLM capability such as how models handle numerical context. It also includes human-centered studies and mechanistic interpretability.

I come from an unconventional background that heavily informs my approach to research. I began my professional life as a **classical guitarist** and instructor, eventually transitioning into public service at the San Antonio Central Library. While there, I managed procurement for **Banned Books Week** and experienced the real-world impact of information access—most notably helping a disabled patron call his mother for the first time in two years. 

Driven by the desire to build better information systems, I taught myself to code while working two jobs. This led me to an **M.S. in Data Analytics**, where I met my advisor, Dr. Anthony Rios, who convinced me to pursue a Ph.D. and join the UTSA NLP Lab.

### **Research Focus**
My current work centers on **Context-Specific Reasoning**, specifically probing how LLMs process varied data types like time series and temporal data. I am equally interested in **Mechanistic Interpretability**—understanding the internal "why" behind model behavior—and **Human-AI Collaboration**, where I study how users (particularly students) perceive and identify LLM agents in live, collaborative settings.
